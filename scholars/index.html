MLK Boston will be sponsoring three contests for students in Boston. The winners of each contest will receive a $10,000 college scholarship, and the three runners up for each contest will receive $2500 scholarships.

Winners will be selected by May 1st, 2018. Stay tuned for information about where and when the winners will be announced.

Oratory Contest

Students will submit a video of themself delivering a memorized two minute excerpt from one of MLK's speeches. The winner of this contest will get an opportunity to deliver the speech at the MLK Boston Gala (details forthcoming). We will select a Boston minister who will assemble a team and process for evaluating all submissions, and then selecting the winner and three runners up.

Essay Contest

Students will submit a 1,000 word essay with a specific idea of set of ideas that MLK would want us to work on in Boston today. The winner and three runners up will have their essay published on our website. (Can we also publish at the Library of Congress?) Brandon Terry (Harvard) will assemble a team and process for evaluating the essay submissions and selecting the winners.

Art Contest

Students will submit a high quality photograph of a painting or drawing they have made with the subject being related to any of Dr. King's work. The winner and three runners up will have their artwork published on our website, with the original art also being displayed at the Boston Public Library. A member of our Art Committee will assemble a process and team for evaluating all submitted works and for selecting the winner and three runners up.
