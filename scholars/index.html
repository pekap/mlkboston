In order to consider the work and legacies of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., and Coretta Scott King in contemporary contexts, MLK Boston sponsor three contests for middle and high school students in Massachusetts. The winners of each contest will receive a $10,000 college scholarship, and the three runners- up for each contest will receive $2500 scholarships.

Winners will be selected by May 1st, 2018. Further information will be forthcoming about where and when the winners will be announced.

Oratory Contest

Students will submit videos of themselves delivering memorized two-minute excerpts from one of MLK's speeches. The winner of this contest will have an opportunity to deliver the speech at the MLK Boston Gala (details forthcoming). Reverend Jeffrey Brown (Twelfth Baptist Church, Roxbury) will assemble a team and develop a process for evaluating submissions to select the winner and three runnersup.

Essay Contest

Students will submit 1,000-word essays addressing a specific idea or set of ideas that Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., and Coretta Scott King would want us to work on in Boston today. The winner and three runners-up will have their essay published on our website. (Can we also publish at the Library of Congress?) Dr. Brandon Terry (Harvard) will assemble a team and develop a process for evaluating the essay submissions and selecting the winner and three runners-up.

Art Contest

Students will submit - high-quality digital images of artworks (such as but not limited to, painting, drawing or sculpture) they have made with the subject being related to any of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. and Coretta Scott Kingâ€™s work. The artist will also submit a brief statement to accompany the artwork. The winner and three runners-up will have their artworks published on our website, with the original art also being displayed at the Boston Public Library. A member of our Art Committee will assemble a team and develop a process for evaluating the submitted artworks and for selecting the winner and three runners-up.

Eligibility

Any public or private middle or high school student in Massachusetts can submit their work for these contests.
